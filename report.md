# Detailed Report on Stage 3 Lung Cancer and its Treatment Options in Singapore\n\n## Executive Summary\n\nThis report provides a comprehensive overview of the current state of lung cancer treatment in Singapore, with a focus on patients with stage 3 lung cancer. It covers various aspects, including the cost of treatment, insurance coverage, and available treatment options. The report also highlights the importance of early diagnosis and treatment, as well as the need for a holistic approach to managing the condition.\n\n## Introduction\n\nLung cancer is a significant health concern in Singapore, with the disease being the most frequent cause of cancer death in males and the second most frequent in females. The cost of lung cancer treatment in Singapore can be expensive, with medical insurance potentially not being enough to cover the costs.\n\n## Treatment Options for Stage 3 Lung Cancer\n\nPatients with stage 3 lung cancer may have other treatment options available to them. Palliative treatment is not the only option to manage the condition, and individuals may be able to receive additional treatment.\n\n## Insurance Coverage\n\nVarious insurance policies are available in Singapore to cover the costs of lung cancer treatment. GREAT SupremeHealth is a health insurance product that offers coverage for medical bills if a child is hospitalized, with a co-payment subject to a maximum of $3,000 per Period of Insurance for eligible bills incurred under Panel Provider or at Restructured Hospital.\n\n## Cost of Treatment\n\nThe cost of lung cancer treatment in Singapore varies depending on the treatment required. Biopsies can cost on average SGD 725 in public hospitals and up to SGD 5,479 in private facilities, while surgeries can cost SGD 3,194 in public hospitals and up to SGD 28,108 in private facilities. Chemotherapy can range from SGD 1,142 to SGD 31,703 in some instances.\n\n## Conclusion\n\nIn conclusion, patients with stage 3 lung cancer in Singapore have various treatment options available to them. It is essential to have a holistic approach to managing the condition, including early diagnosis and treatment. Insurance coverage can help alleviate some of the financial burdens associated with treatment costs.\n\n## Recommendations\n\n1. Early diagnosis and treatment are crucial in managing lung cancer.\n2. A holistic approach to treatment, including palliative care, should be adopted.\n3. Insurance coverage can help alleviate some of the financial burdens associated with treatment costs.\n\n\n**Medical Insurance Policies in Singapore**\n\n| Policy Name | Coverage |\n| --- | --- |\n| GREAT SupremeHealth | Covers up to 95% of total hospitalisation bill for respiratory diseases, including COVID-19 |\n| FWD Cancer Insurance | 100% payout for all stages of cancer diagnosis, with multiple 100% payouts for subsequent new or recurring late-stage cancer diagnosis |\n| AIA Absolute Critical Cover | Covers 175 conditions, including major cancers, with a Power Relapse Benefit that offers up to 200% coverage for five critical illnesses |\n| GREAT Critical Cover Series | Covers 53 critical illnesses or just the top 3 critical illness conditions - cancer, heart attack, or stroke |
